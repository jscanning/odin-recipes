Pasta Carbonara

Description

This bacon carbonara is a super rich, classic "bacon and egg" pasta dish that's great to serve for company. This recipe also makes an unusual brunch offering.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pasta or your preferred noodle pasta
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, as needed
  • 8 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 4 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Garnish: 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook pasta in boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes. Drain, toss pasta with 1 tablespoon olive oil, and set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, place diced bacon in a large skillet over medium heat; cook and stir until evenly browned, about 10 minutes. Drain bacon on paper towels, reserving 2 tablespoons bacon fat in the skillet.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon olive oil to bacon fat in the skillet. Add chopped onion and cook over medium heat until onion is translucent. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add wine and cook 1 minute more.
  4. Return cooked bacon to the skillet; add cooked spaghetti. Toss to coat and heat through, adding more olive oil if it seems dry or sticks together. Add beaten eggs and cook, tossing constantly with tongs or a large fork, until eggs are barely set. Quickly add 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese and toss again. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve warm with chopped parsley sprinkled on top and extra Parmesan cheese at the table.
